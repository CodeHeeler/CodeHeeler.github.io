Learning

My love for learning all things, especially science, was cultivated at a very early age, and I was especially lucky to have parents who supported and nourished that learning. Not everyone is so privileged, and thus I would like to help kids to gain a love for learning to code through camps and community projects to better prepare them for a career path in STEM, should they choose to pursue one. To that end, I'm currently looking into area groups that teach through the Raspberry Pi but am certainly open to other ideas.

Learning continues at all ages, whether that's for a career change as was the case for me, adding new languages and skills, or even just working on a fun hobby project. Finding resources has never been easier with internet searches and videos at your fingertips, but there's still great value in attending Meetup groups in your area and connecting to other coders for support or inspiration. Being one of those coders, feel free to reach out to me, @CodeHeeler, on Twitter where I share educational resources, interesting articles, and some of my own passions like linguistics and pixel art.

The goal is to develop a more in-depth resource guide and event calendar here in time, so stay tuned, and in the meanwhile, hit me up on Twitter anytime!

Sharing

One of the aims closest to my heart is sustainable agriculture, better known as Slow Food or the Local Food Movement. I'm also a huge foodie and love encouraging others to experiment with world cuisine through food trucks, restaurants, and festivals. Familiarity is key to helping newcomers to feel welcomed and comfortable, and websites or social media can be great gateways to know your farmer and support local businesses. Unfortunately, I've found a number of my favorite farms and restaurants lack websites.

In order to share my coding skills to better my community, I will be offering to create simple websites for local farms, small businesses, and non-profits. If you're interested in learning more, or even have a request for a coding project other than a website (basic inventory systems, or a database for tracking herd or crop information, etc), please email me, CodeHeeler, at gmail dot com. Also, if you're a coder yourself, please join me and offer to provide services in your own community!

Joining

Being a passionate supporter of open source, I'm looking forward to becoming more involved in a number of projects worldwide. From the gamified habit tracker, Habitica, to smaller projects asking for help on GitHub, my interests are varied and learning new things is a welcome challenge. The difficulty is in getting started when others frequently are assigned an issue before you've had a chance to jump in. Thankfully, there are newsletters and twitter feeds devoted to helping newcomers get acquainted with open source projects.

Check out my Twitter feed for some of these guides if you'd like to participate, and if you'd like to share projects I might not be aware of but could join, reach out to me on GitHub--I am CodeHeeler on either.
